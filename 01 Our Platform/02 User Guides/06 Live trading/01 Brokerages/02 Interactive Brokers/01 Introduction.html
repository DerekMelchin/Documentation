What is IB:
-"Interactive Brokers (IB) is a low-cost provider of trade execution and clearing services for individuals, advisors, prop trading groups, brokers, and hedge funds. IB's premier technology provides direct access to stocks, options, futures, forex, bonds, and funds on over 100 markets worldwide from a single IB Universal account. Member NYSE, FINRA, SIPC." // from docs v1
-Interactive Brokers was founded by Thomas Peterffy in 1993 with the goal to "create technology to provide liquidity on better terms. Compete on price, speed, size, diversity of global products and advanced trading tools". Interactive Brokers provides access to trading Equities, ETFs, Options, Futures, Future Options, Forex, Gold, Warrants, Bonds, and Mutual Funds for clients in over 200 countries and territories [source] with no minimum deposit. IB also provides paper trading, a trading platform, and educational services.

What asset classes are supported:
-Equity, Options, Futures, Future Options

What order types are supported:
-market, limit, stop market, stop limit, market on open, market on close, limit if touched

What time in force is supported:
-GoodTilCanceledTimeInForce
-DayTimeInForce
-GoodTilDateTimeInForce

What account types are available:
-Cash, margin

What leverage is available:
-Cash accounts: 1x
-Margin accounts:
   -Equity: 2x
   -Options: 1x
   -Futures: 1x
   -FutureOptions: 1x

What are the fees (InteractiveBrokersFeeModel):
-Equity: $0.005/share, $1 minimum fee
-Options:  https://github.com/QuantConnect/Lean/blob/1c3d849ad5b35ca32b0f030b518e8ed8bf86f733/Common/Orders/Fees/InteractiveBrokersFeeModel.cs#L202-L235
   - Option exercise: $0
-Futures: $1.85/contract
-Future Options: $1.85/contract

What buying power model is used:
- Equity: SecurityMarginModel
- Options: OptionMarginModel
- Futures: FutureMarginModel
- FutureOptions: FuturesOptionsMarginModel

What margin call model is used:
- DefaultMarginCallModel

What settlement model is used:
- Cash accounts:
    - Equity & Options: DelayedSettlementModel
- Otherwise: ImmediateSettlementModel

What support is there for Financial Advisor accounts:
-Requires Trading Firm or Institution organization
-It gets the balance + positions from the master account, not the individual groups/accounts
-Places orders based on what it has in the master account and IB places the orders accordingly
-These certified professionals can use a single trading algorithm to manage several clients accounts.

Special cases / Limitations:
-You can only have 1 connection at a time to your account
-Refer to Data feeds > IB
- ETFs might need special permission to be traded. EU residents can't trade them.
